# Trauma

## trauma

In addition to social interactions, travel, and combat, characters will likely face physical and mental trauma during their adventures. Most physical trauma affects the body, and most mental trauma, the mind. Some instances can damage both.

## Physical Trauma

Perhaps the more common of the two types, physical trauma is divided into three categories:

Fatigue : an overall lack of energy that can result from arduous travel, lengthy battles, and spellcasting. Such exhaustion accumulates as fatigue, a penalty applied to almost all tests. It usually subsides with rest and sleep, but weakness fatigue can last throughout lingering ailments.

Injury : direct and more life-threatening damage to the body. Injuries manifest as specific states and effects, including bleeding, shock, and impairment.

## Mental Trauma

Often as debilitating as bodily damage, mental trauma manifests in three ways:

Fear : a reaction to perceived danger, most commonly arising from the threat of violence. Characters rely on will, reasoning, and experience to withstand fear, whether responding to the stress of combat or resisting intimidation. Fear usually comes and goes quickly.

Certain strange sources of fear affect folk more intensely. Characters might face such aberrance from ghosts or bizarre beasts.

Stress : a longer-lasting response to severe trauma. The mental equivalent of physical injury, such stress can leave a character's psyche altered, sometimes permanently. The most frightening type of stress-the dreaded Shadow of Bukrai-can even rend a character's soul.

Ailments : physical trauma other than fatigue and injury. This includes disease, malnutrition, poison, and conditions such as heat exhaustion and cold exposure.

Aural Shock : the temporary disruption of the character's connection to the spirit world. While not permanent, Aural Shock prevents Esoteric Group skill and Aura tests.
