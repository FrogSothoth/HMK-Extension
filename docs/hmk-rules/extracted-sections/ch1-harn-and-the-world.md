Three days to sail the swift Emel Straits was not a long time, but ten years since having been on a ship will leave her feeling the swells for a while. After a quick nod of thanks to the captain up on deck, Nolora turns to find herself suddenly impeded by a swarm of milling sailors along the wharf.

Memories return of the brief journey in her youth to this same Cherafir port-the web of thick cordage, the din of shouts and conversation in a dozen tongues, the wide-ranging canvas of folk from all realms of Venarive. Jostled backwards by the crowd further onto the pier, Nolora finally sees what the throng is: the boarding crew of the immense three-masted merchant galley berthed opposite the vessel she just left. Secured stern first to the wharf, the Larumar's length juts over a hundred feet into the water beyond, where mooring lines from a handful of small oared boats steady its prow for the anticipated row out to sea.

Nolora grips her thick bow stave for leverage in making headway ashore through the embarking sailors. While not menacing, they part absently, Nolora not hesitating to lend a subtle elbow to her progress. She pauses at hearing a greeting called out in her native Emelan. Ahead, emerging out of the crowd, appears Elben, robed now in finer silk and leather than she recalls from before, the corners of his eyes more creased, the hair at his temples more greyed. He repeats her name while extending a hand, and says: 'Welcome back to the Misty Isle!-  it's been far too long.'
