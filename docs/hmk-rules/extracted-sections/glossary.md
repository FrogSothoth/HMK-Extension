# Keléstia Glossary

- aberrance a strange or grotesque source of fear ; harder to resist and usually	more	stressful.
- active when	a	soul	can	take spirit	actions	and	be	sensed	by living entities; one of three soul conditions ( dissolved , dormant ).
- ailment physical	trauma	other	than injury or fatigue ,	such	as	disease, malnutrition,	and	poisoning.
- anchored when	a	being's	soul	is connected	to	its	body	by	the	Aura attribute	(the	'anchor').
- animation when	the	personality	of a spirit with no natural shape gains control of an object .	If	the	object has	Ego,	then	animation	requires successful	spirit	conflict.
- appearance what	a	being's	physique looks	like,	including	comeliness, build	(height	and	weight),	skin complexion,	eye	colour,	hair	colour.
- arcane mysteries endeavours that directly	draw	on	the	principle energies	of	reality.	All	function through	the	Aura	attribute.
- arcanist a practitioner of an arcane mystery,	such	as	a	mage,	invoker, alchemist,	diviner,	or	summoner.
- artefact an object of wonderous ability;	can	be	arcane	(minor,	major, rune piece); astral (talisman); or divine	(relic).
- astral a	being	who	temporarily inhabits its active soul in the astralscape of the spirit world; distinct from an astralist .
- astral journey the	temporary experience an astral has of sensing and	exploring	the	spirit	world.
- astral mysteries endeavours that beseech	spirit	entities	for	power.
- astralist a practitioner of an astral mystery,	such	as	a	shaman.
- astralscape the expression of the spirit	world	sensed	by	an	active	soul with	the	Empathy	attribute.
- attunement (also attune , attuned ) a connection between a living entity's	soul	and	certain	artefacts.
- Aura strength of soul; in beings Aura anchors	the	soul	to	the	body,	which is	the	definition	of	being	alive.
- aural shock temporary	disruption	of the	connection	between	a	being's soul	and	body;	one	of	three	types	of mental trauma ( fear , stress ).
- aware when a being is conscious and cognisant; able to test Awareness;	includes	being	stunned.
- beast a	being	whose	personality includes Aura and Will but not Morality.
- being a	living	entity	with	a	soul anchored	to	a	shape,	such	as	people, beasts,	plants,	or	Will-artefacts.

## keléstia  glossary

body the corporeal shape a being inhabits	in	the	physical	world.

- bound form the shape an elemental inhabits	in	the	physical	world, comprised	of	a	naturally	occurring element,	such	as	flame	or	water.

dormant when	a	being's	soul	is uninhabited,	yet	may	still	be	sensed by	living	entities;	it	may	partake	in Spirit	Conflict	only	when	targeted by	an	active	soul;	one	of	three	soul conditions ( active , dissolved ).

build a	living	being's	height	and weight,	either	quantified	literally	or qualified	through	descriptive	traits.

captive soul a dormant soul in Morgathian hell ( Durakhar ) that cannot become active; souls outside of hell cannot engage it with	spirit	actions.

- conscious when	a	being	is	awake in	the	landscape	of	the	physical world;	includes	being	awake	but unaware .
- concurrence (also concur , concurred ) an esoteric connection between a soul	and	an	Earthmaster	object.
- consonance the bond Aura creates between a being and carried objects	up	to	half	Spirit	ML	lb.
- continuous effect an enchantment that	is	always	active;	invocation	is not	required.
- control when	a	personality	decides how	its	shape	(usually	body)	acts.
- corporeal an	entity	inhabiting	a shape	in	the	physical	world	that can touch and be touched;  includes body and incarnated form .
- corpse the	object	a	body	turns	into when Aura is no longer anchored to the	body.
- creature a	living	entity	with	a	body and at least an Aura and Will; includes	people	and	beasts.
- damage the	weakening	of	an object's	durability.
- death when Aura no longer anchors a	being's	soul	to	the	body,	the	latter of	which	usually	temporarily	exists as an object ( corpse ).
- dematerialise when	an	entity	loses its	shape	in	the	physical	world;	it can	still	technically	inhabit	a	body, just	one	that	is	not	'there'.
- destruction when an object is beyond	function	and	repair.

disembodiment when a being switches inhabitation and perspective	from	the	physical	world to the spirit world; the soul remains anchored to either a corporeal or dematerialised	body.

- dissolved (also dissolution )  when a soul	can	neither	take	spirit	actions nor	be	sensed	by	living	entities;	one of three soul conditions ( active , dormant ).
- divine mysteries endeavours that assume the existence of gods but the outcomes of which do not demonstrate	or	prove	it.
- Earthmaster objects the objects thought	to	have	been	created	by the ancient Earthmasters; believed by	some	to	be	an	unknown	type	of living	entity.
- Ego the mental attribute in an artificial	personality	that	performs the same functions as Aura and Will do	in	natural	personalities.
- element what	principle	energy	is	in the	physical	world	landscape.
- energy (also force )  a feature of Keléstia with neither a soul nor a shape;	comprised	of	three	typesprinciple , place ,	and gateway .
- entity a feature of Keléstia with a soul	and/or	shape,	living	( spirit or being ) or non-living ( object ).
- esoterica the arcane and astral mysteries;	so	grouped	by	their reliance on the Aura attribute and by	their	overt	expressions	(spells and powers) versus the enigmatic expressions	of	the	divine	mysteries.
- ethereal a	living	entity	inhabiting its soul and having perspective in the spirit world (a spirit or astral ); also	synonymous	with	the	term spirit world ('ethereal	world').
- ether-corporeal a spirit inhabiting the	spirit	and	physical	worlds	at	the same	time,	due	to	a	rift	between the	astral-	and	landscapes.
- fate a	living	entity's	life	course	as	an element of timeless Keléstia; the primary	focus	of	divination.
- fate points the	quanta	players	may use	to	better	define	the	fate	of	their characters as revealed through attribute	and	skill	tests	and	rolls; not applicable to tests of Aura and skills	in	the	Esoteric	Group.
- fatigue exhaustion	or	lack	of	vitality; one	of	three	types	of	physical trauma ( ailment , injury ).
- fear an	immediate,	usually	fleeting, response to perceived danger; one of	three	types	of	mental	trauma ( aural shock , stress ).
- feature one of two components of Keléstia,	either entity or energy .
- folk a	being	with	Morality	and capable of individual purpose ; this likely	excludes	Gargun,	who	have likely	only	a	group/species	purpose.
- form the corporeal shape an incarnated spirit inhabits in the physical	world;	usually	functions like	a	body	with	regard	to	trauma, except	upon	suffering	a	KIA	shock state,	when	it	is	destroyed.
- gateway a	type	of	energy	that transfers	beings,	usually	from	one point	in	a	physical	world	to	another point	in	a	physical	world.
- grace a	bond	made	by	an	Aspect ('god')	with	living	entities	that allows them to request miracles; an unfathomable	divine	mystery.
- host mass the natural element that houses	an	Elemental's	bound	form in	the	physical	world;	must	be	at least	as	large	as	the	bound	form.
- incantation (also incanter )  the arcane mystery of channelling ethereal	forces	into	the	physical world; includes invocation and spellcasting	(such	as	Pvarism).
- incarnation (also incarnate ,	-d ) a spirit's	ability	to	take	shape	in	the physical	world,	called	taking form .
- incorporeal (also noncorporeal )  not having	shape	in	the	physical	world. inert a	physical	world	shape	unable to	take	action	on	its	own,	including a	living,	but	uninhabited,	body.
- inhabitation (also inhabit )  where an entity's personality exists-either in	its	soul	or,	if	it	has	one,	its	shape (which	is	a	body,	if	a	being;	or	a form,	if	an	incarnated	spirit).
- injury potentially	life-threatening trauma	to	a	being's	body	or	form; can	include	bleeding,	shock,	and impairment;	one	of	three	types	of physical	trauma	( ailment, fatigue ).
- inoperable when an object, or part of	one,	does	not	function.
- intensity one of three levels of psyche	trauma;	a	trait,	impulse,	or disorder.
- invisible when a shape cannot be seen	in	the	physical	world	but might	be	sensed	in	other	ways (touched,	smelled,	heard).
- Keléstia reality;	composed	of	two features-entities	and	energy.
- landscape the expression of the physical	world	directly	sensed with the Perception attribute (or subattributes)	and	Awareness	skill.
- latent effect an enchantment that requires	invocation	to	be	active.
- living entity an	entity	with	a	natural soul,	either	a spirit or a being .
- manifestation the	ability	of	a	spirit inhabiting its soul in the spirit world	to	create	sensory	phenomena in	the	physical	world.
- medium an arcanist inhabiting a body	in	the	physical	world	who can	take	the	sense,	commune,	and conflict	actions	in	the	spirit	world.
- mental attributes Aura,	Will, Reasoning,	Creativity,	Empathy, Eloquence; can be part of a living entity's	personality.

mental trauma the	weakening	of	a living	entity's	personality;	includes fear , stress ,	and aural shock .

Morality a characteristic that measures	selflessness;	unique	to natural personalities and some artefacts with Ego; required for the entity	to	have	a purpose .

morality trait a description of the character's	morality;	often	just	a single	adjective	for	shorthand.

native soul a being inhabiting its body	in	the	physical	world	with	a dormant	soul	in	the	spirit	world.

natural an inherent characteristic of an	entity.

mute a	personality	inhabiting	its body	but	not	able	to	control	it	or speak.

non-living entity an	entity	with no	natural	soul	and	usually	no personality;	an	artefact	with	Ego volition has an artificial personality and	so	is	still	non-living.

object a	non-living	entity	that naturally	lacks personality ; when enchanted	with	Ego,	it	gains	an artificial	personality	that	inhabits	a shape	called	an	arcane	artefact.

person (also people )  a being whose soul	is	anchored	to	a	natural	body and	who	possesses	Morality.

personality what	inhabits	an	entity's soul	or	body,	either	natural	(based on	Aura	and	Will)	or	artificial (based on Ego); can include mental attributes	and	skills,	morality, purpose,	grace,	psyche,	and	fate.

personality trait a description of	a	living	entity's	personality; three	common	typessunsign , morality ,	and psyche traits -are	often	described	by	a	single adjective	for	shorthand.

perspective where	a	being's	senses are directed-at the landscape of	the	physical	world,	or	at	the astralscape	of	the	spirit	world.

petition when a shaman in the astralscape	seeks	power	from	a spirit guide .

physical attributes Strength, Endurance,	Dexterity,	Agility, Perception,	Comeliness;	Voice;	can be	part	of	a	being's	physique.

physical trauma the	weakening	of	a being's	physique;	includes fatigue , injury ,	and ailment .

physical world one of two parts of reality,	where	entities	exist	in	a landscape.

physique the	physical	attributes	and appearance associated with a living entity's	shape.

piety a characteristic of the divine mysteries;	measures	the	reverence

place energy ethereal phenomena that	exist	in	the	spirit	and	physical worlds	at	the	same	time,	often associated	with	specific principle energies .

plants a	being	whose	personality includes	Aura,	however	faint,	but not	Will	and	Morality;	sometimes confused	with	a	mere	'object'.

possession when	the	personality	of a spirit with no natural shape gains control of	a	being's	body	through successful	spirit	conflict.

principle energy ethereal phenomena natural to the spirit world that are integral to the existence	of	entities;	exists	in	many, perhaps	infinite	varieties;	Pvarism focuses on six core manifestations (earth,	metal,	fire,	air,	aura,	water) and	various	associated	principles.

psyche the	personality	characteristic that	reflects	a	living	entity's	mental health.

psyche stress level the quanta that measure	trauma	to	the	psyche, similar	to	how	injury	levels measure	trauma	to	the	body.

psyche trait the	personality	traits that	arise	out	of	psyche	stress; qualified	by	one	of	three	intensity levels:	trait,	impulse,	and	disorder.

purpose a	personality	trait	that	can only	exist	in	entities	with	Morality; can be a one-word description but usually	requires	longer	explication; a	central	element	players	develop in	their	characters	during	the	game.

reality the English translation of the word Keléstia .

reembodiment when an astral switches embodiment from the soul	to	the	body,	and	perspective from	the	spirit	world	to	the	physical.

rematerialise when	an	entity	that has dematerialised regains its shape	in	the	physical	world.	Often accompanied	by	reembodiment.

reverie an	astral	mystery	practiced by	the	Ælrhin	(elven-folk);	involves a semi-conscious state similar to shamanic trance but does not require	an	astral	journey.

semi-corporeal when a being inhabiting	a	body	in	the	physical world	exhibits	incorporeal	features, such as translucence; sometimes the being can also gain perspective in	the	spirit	world.

senses how	an	entity	achieves perspective	of	the	physical	world landscape	(touch,	taste,	hearing, sight,	smell,	thermoception	etc.), or the spirit world astralscape (feelings); Perception attribute and Awareness	skill	govern	the	former, Empathy	attribute	the	latter.

Shadow an ethereal phenomenon associated with the god Morgath; can	induce	fell	trauma	to	a	being's soul;	not	well	understood.

shadow life the	'reward'	for embracing	the	Shadow.

shape how	an	entity	appears	in	the landscape	of	the	physical	world: some	spirits	take	incarnated	form; Elementals	take	bound	form; beings	have	a	body;	non-living entities (objects) are simply	shape.

Shock the	skill	measuring	the vitality	of	a	being's	body	and	how well	it	resists	physical	trauma.

soul what	a	living	entity's personality	inhabits	in	the	spirit world; also called its true form .

spirit a	living	entity	with	a	natural soul	but	without	a	natural	shape.

spirit actions what an active soul can do in the astralscape; includes roaming,	sensing,	communing, immersion,	and	conflict.

Spirit the	skill	measuring	the vitality	of	a	living	entity's	soul	and how	well	it	resists	spells,	mental trauma,	and	spirit	conflict.

spirit guides the target of a shaman's petition ; either ancestor or	totem	spirit,	or	energy	'spirit'.

spirit world one of two parts of reality;	where	souls	and	energy exist	in	the	astralscape.

sunsign the zodiacal constellation under which a person is born; affects	several	arcane	tasks,	such	as spellcasting	and	fate	rolls.

sunsign trait a	personality	trait that	reflects	the	influence	of	a character's	time	of	birth.

tradition a	specific	shamanic heritage; those not part of the tradition	are	called	outsiders.

trauma the	weakening	of	an	entity's mental	or	physical	health.

true form synonymous	with	a	living entity's soul ; it can be sensed in an active or dormant soul in the spirit world,	and	revealed	through	a being's	Aura	in	the	physical	world.

true sight the	various	ways	a	living entity	senses	another's true form .

unanchored when	a	being's	Aura no longer connects the soul to the body;	results	in	death	of	the	body and	the	personality's	immediate inhabitation	of	the	soul.

unaware an unconscious being,	or one conscious but	not	cognisant, unable to test Awareness; includes the	Incapacitated	shock	state.

unconscious when a being is not awake	and	does	not	perceive	the landscape	of	the	physical	world. volition the Ego or Will of a major artefact that allows it to activate two	or	more	enchantments.

Will a mental attribute in a natural personality;	can	serve	as volition in	a	major	artefact.

stress trauma	that	alters	a	being's psyche ; one of three mental traumas ( aural shock , fear ).

Will-artefact a	natural	personality inhabiting	an	artefact-object;	thus, a	being	but	not	a	person.
