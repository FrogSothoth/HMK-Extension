## modes of adventure

Characters on Kethira find adventure in many ways. They can wield social influence, embark on great journeys, and engage in dangerous battles, to name a few. Though most exploits combine several such modes, they each have unique game rules. The Adventure chapter explains these in detail, enabling players to game in a wide variety of situations throughout Kethira's many places.

## Influence

Most Kethiran cultures are far from egalitarian. While negotiating their restrictions can prove quite difficult for the underclasses, even the higher classes cannot often express power without some risk. Adventures of influence encompass a number of actions, including mercantile trades, timely missions of diplomacy, and the resolution of conflicting desire.

- A fur trader travels to a remote outpost in the alpine Nethil river valley to barter with a familiar trapper, whose rare pelts will bring certain profit in the coming spring fair. Located at the wilderness nexus of violent nomadic Gargun and rival Anoa tribesmen, the meeting could prove unwise.
- A priestess of Halea navigates carefully the opulent halls of her temple in Rykal, a powerful city state in the southern island chain of Karejia. Serving the Mistress of Discipline, she brokers in the lucrative secrets of voluble patrons and ambitious senior clergy.

## Travel

The three continents of Kethira consist mostly of vast swathes of wilderness. Even the regions more densely settled, such as northwest Venarive, remain predominantly uncultivated and seldom traversed without great care. Ancient ruins and Earthmaster sites lie forgotten in lands not so remote from the few centres of trade. Adventures are had simply by looking for these places-and certainly so upon finding them.

- In the northern tundra and mountain ranges at the frigid crown of eastern Venarive, the Great Altic nomadic tribal people tend vast herds of reindeer. They hold sacred the surrounding peaks of the Muyarh Mountains, sending thither their wisest to brave the ice and commune with ancestors.
- A group of Zhelorians drive a train of buffalo-drawn carts along the jungle trails of central Anzeloria. Headed to the remote city of Koanto-inaccessible to sizeable river-trade-the party of merchants, teamsters, and guards seek fortune in the transport of valuable metals back to the riverhead at N'dalla, over a hundred miles behind them.

## the mysteries

Kethira expresses powerful, yet rare and guarded magic; its gods, by contrast, remain aloof and unmanifest. The arcane, astral, and divine mysteries add monumental texture to the exploits of intrigue, voyage, and conflict.

## artefacts

Both the magic objects crafted by arcanists and the enigmatic tools left by the ancient Earthmasters inspire quests throughout all of Kethira. As exceptional as their makers, most artefacts exhibit unique powers.

## the spirit world

Perhaps the most dangerous adventures do not even take place in the physical world. Journeys to and from the ethereal planes remain clouded in legend in many cultures, save notably for tribal peoples who practice the esoteric shamanic traditions.

## Combat

Combat often arises out of a consequential downturn in the previous two types of adventure-a catastrophic failure of influence leads to a vengeful knife in the dark; a rash misstep in navigation springs a sudden ambush along a narrow trail. Violence can find characters in many other ways, too-as lords raiding neighbouring manors, as vikings marauding rich shipments. Even more frightfully, Kethira harbours the lairs of myriad unnatural beasts, the might of whom is rarely tested.

- An army of the Kingdom of Palithane laid siege to the Trierzi town of Waleden last autumn in revenge for an earlier attack on two of its border keeps. Before the spring thaw and the siege's renewal, the Trierzi defenders wish to send a messenger to a nearby village beyond the invested walls.

## Trauma

The challenge of facing physical and mental trauma arises out of many situations. Arrow wounds and concussion, disease and the hardships of weather, psyche stress and the precarious tether of one's soul. All are potential threats to the adventurer.
