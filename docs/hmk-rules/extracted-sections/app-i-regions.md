## Realms

Region tables list in rows their various constituent realms ,	whether kingdom,	city-state,	tribal	range,	or	some	other	political	or	cultural area.	The	columns	describe	each	realm	by	Society,	Pantheon,	and Native	Language	(given	first)	and	Regional	Languages	(after	the	bold bullet, · ).	Dialects	(page	424)	are	in	italics	following	the	language.

Special d100 :	the	d100	results	in	the	first	column	of	a	Region	table generally	reflect	each	realm's	population.	Several	entries	require	an additional	d10	roll	to	determine	randomly	the	realm.	For	instance, rolling 98 with the d100 on the Azeryan Region table	(next	page), requires a subsequent d10 roll: 1 ( Gothmir Gares March );	2-7	( Gothmir Loas March ); 8 ( Kalinelari March );	9-0	( Tonatri Lands ).

Region Notes : the right column on each page lists special information	related	to	the	various	Region	tables,	which	are	derived from	and	meant	to	accompany Venârivè: Northwestern L´ythia and Venârivè Map .	Refer	to	these	publications	for	greater	detail.

Elder Folk Regions :	each	of	the	four	PC	Elder	folk	given	in	this	volume has	its	own	special	Region	table	(page	423).	The Malák and Mizúni Class &amp; Occupation tables	are	located	on	this	page	as	well.

## áltôr notes

- The	town	of	Amlacht	is	split	evenly	between	the	founding valley	Alts	and	the	Ivinians	who	arrived	around tr 640.	The two	peoples	are	on	friendly	terms.
- Lybath	has	an	Altic	majority	(80%)/Ivinian	minority	(20%).
- For	the	West	and	East	Alti	lands,	generate	the	specific	tribal type	by	rolling	d100:	01-20	(forest);	21-60	(great);	61-80 (valley);	81-00	(water).	See	also	dialect	note	E	(page	424).
- The	Alti	worship	a	pantheon	of	six	gods,	akin	to	the	'Old Gods'	(page	343): Maadtera (Ylvyr), Syra (Beona), Juoksa (Elarana), Arak (Garanik), Manna (Ziem),	and Rouhtta (Mordar).

## ázeryàn notes

- The	peoples	of	the	Empire	of	Azeryan	speak	a	dozen dialects	of	Nasik	Azeri,	or	'Low	Azeryani'.	The	several dialects	spoken	around	the	Venarian	sea,	including	those on	the	east	coast	of	Hepekeria	and	in	Byria,	express	varying levels	of	mutual	intelligibility	(note	C,	page	424).
- A	character	who	receives	5	DP	as	a	native	speaker	in	any dialect	of	Nasik	Azeri,	from	the	page	47	sidebar	table,	also receives	4	DP	in	native	Zakimladal,	or	'High	Azeryani',	a complex	language	of	the	educated	and	upper	classes.	Under unique	circumstances,	lesser	class	Azeryani	characters and	foreigners	may	develop	Zakimladal	(foreign	scholars quite	often	do	so).	Native	speakers	of	other	Azeri	languages receive	the	same-family	Foreign	Language	Boost	(page 48	sidebar),	as	usual.	However,	because	of	its	exclusivity, Zakimladal	does	not	appear	as	a	Regional	Language.
- Azeryan	abandoned	its	eastern	Marches	to	the	Reksyni horse warriors in tr 660.	The	Reksyni	now	dominate	(80% population)	in	the	Gothmir	Gares	and	Kalinelari	Marches, whereas	the	Azeryani	do	so	(95%)	in	Gothmir	Loas	March.
- The Tonatri are an isolated mountain tribal people (regarding	their	language,	see	sidebar	note	on	page	425).
- The	Lankor	Emhle	are	a	group	of	the	aligned	tribal	people	of the	Cult	of	the	Covenant	(page	343).

## b´yria notes

- Byria's	Varanian/Laranian	theocracy	tolerates	other	worship of	only	Eoni/Peoni,	Halea,	Save-K'nor,	and	Zarath.
- Byrian	is	a	distant	dialect	of	Nasik	Azeri	(note	C,	page	424).

## chogôro &amp; mafán notes

- The region of Mafan shown on the map consists of the peninsulas,	coasts,	and	outer	islands,	as	well	as	the northern land that stretches around the Gulf of Mafan to the	east,	technically	outside	of	Venarive.
- The Chogori dominate the inland portions of the states in the	central	section	(dialect	information	forthcoming).
- The Pechalari and Shenti are found in the far western states of	the	region	(dialect	information	forthcoming).

## dalkésh notes

- Dalkesh	society	is	caste-based.	Barring	circumstances	of adoption	or	marriage,	all	native	characters	living	in	Dalkesh must	select	their	Parent	Occupation	as	their	own.
- The	native	language	of	most	within	the	Dalkesh	Empire is	Dalken	(with	caste-based	M1	dialects),	but	a	handful	of other	languages	are	spoken	within	its	borders.	Elbythian dominates in its two most northern provinces (Adesh and	Resikhar),	Karejian	in	the	islands	of	Heponpura,	and Tuvaraneastern in	the	western	Sobrash.	Even	in	these provinces,	Dalken	is	spoken	in	the	imperial	bureaucracy.
- For	Realms	listing	two	native	languages,	speakers	have	an equal	chance	to	have	either.	The	non-native	language	then becomes	a	Regional	Language	for	this	speaker	(page	24).

## emélrenè notes

- The Emhle Range is the core home of the tribal people of the	Cult	of	the	Covenant	(page	343).	The	Jerinalian	group listed here reaches into the northern Palithaner and western Shorkyne	regions.
- The	Emelan	spoken	by	most	folk	throughout	the	Shires exhibits	only	slight	regional	dialects	(M0),	but	its	dialect called	Emhlen,	spoken	by	the	tribal	Emhle,	is	archaic	enough to	give	others	mild	difficulty	(note	F,	page	424).

## hácherdad notes

- Hacherian is a distant dialect of the older Besha language spoken	by	the	people	of	the	Beshakan	Desert	to	the	south (note	J,	page	424).
- Hacherians	value	greatly	literacy;	as	a	mark	of	status	and power,	the	wealthy	often	establish	schools	for	the	poor	to learn	the	Neramic	script.

## huriséa notes

- Hurisea	consists	of	eleven	major	principalities	and	54 minor	ones.	Figend	and	Unten	are	held	by	Ivinian	clans of	Jarenmark	(Ivinia	Region);	Helgen	is	held	by	a	clan	of Ledenheim	(Harbaal	Region);	and	Chegen	and	Ogened	by two	Shorkyni	ruling	houses	(Shorkyne	Region).
- The	Quarphic	pantheon	includes	a	variety	of	spirits	headed overall	by	two	chief	ones,	male	and	female,	respectively associated	with	the	sun	and	the	moon,	and	known	as Anulome	and	Diyel.	Temples	and	rituals	are	relatively organised,	particularly	amongst	the	settled	Hurisean peoples.	There	are	rumours	of	a	dark	cult	devoted	to	a demonic	spirit	known	as	Kurazla.
- The	West	Venarian	and	Ivinian	pantheons	are	worshiped	by the	ruling	elite	where	noted	in	those	principalities.
- Quarphic-w estern shares	great	mutual	intelligibility	with	the Hurisean	of	the	more	settled	interior	(note	M,	page	425).
- The	Shorka,	Ivinian,	and	Harbaaler	languages	are	those spoken	by	the	rulers	of	their	respective	principalities,	with the	Huriseans	native	to	most	non-nobility.

## ivínia notes

- The	Great	Kingdom	of	Seldenbaal	consists	of	five	subkingdoms	(Balisheim	through	Seldenbaal	in	the	table).
- The	Yarili	are	predominantly	shamanic,	but	include	worship of a version of Sarajin and parts of the Old Gods (page 343): Husraal (Ziem)	and	Ylvyr.

## karéjia notes

- While Karejians worship the same Azeri pantheon of Azeryani,	Morgath	is	much	less	popular	than	on	the	Lythian mainland.	Conversely,	the	worship	of	Halea	is	notably stronger	throughout	Karejia,	with	the	theocratic	Helas	citystate	the	seat	of	the	Halean	pontiff.
- Most	Venarians	informally	recognise	their	culture's	entire pantheon.	However,	Karejians	tend	to	venerate	much	more directly	multiple	gods	and	faiths.	To	reflect	this,	Karejian characters receive 1 DP for three separate native religions during	substep	3	of	character	generation	(page	47).
- Each	city-state	expresses	its	own	dialect	of	Karejian	(note	D, page	424).

## shôrk´ynè notes

- The	royal	lands	of	the	king	of	Shorkyne	are	limited	to those	from	his	role	as	the	duke	of	Tharia	(Kemol,	Montivel, and	Quareld	Counties).	The	other	dukes	and	counts	are relatively	independent,	the	most	powerful	of	which	are	the dukes	of	Alagon	and	Ensel.
- The	Shorka	language	is	divided	into	three	broad	dialect groups-coastal,	southern,	and	eastern.	Coastal	and eastern	speakers	share	only	M2,	while	southern	speakers have	M1	with	both	the	other	two	(note	N,	page	425).
- The	Shorkyni	Emhle	lands	are	home	to	the	aligned	tribal people	of	the	Cult	of	the	Covenant	(page	343),	spread	here throughout	the	Jerinalian,	Nadami,	Lankori,	and	Tilame groups	that	also	reach	into	surrounding	regions.

## thónia notes

- Thonia's	varied	ecology	of	rainforest,	savannah,	swampy lakes,	and	soaring	mountains	have	allowed	the	six	dialects of	Thonian	to	remain	quite	distinct,	and,	in	some	instances, nearly	mutually	unintelligible	(note	A,	page	424).
- Natives of the Kingdom of Arlanto are expected to honour Kelenos	above	all	others	of	the	Azeri	Pantheon.
- The	Elanasi	tribal	folk	are	divided	equally	between	a	'wet' coastal	group	and	a	'dry'	herding,	inland	one.
